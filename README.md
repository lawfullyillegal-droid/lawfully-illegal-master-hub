## ‚öñÔ∏è Core Legal Framework

### üí∞ Money Definitions (Medium of Exchange)

**Source:** [`medium-of-exchange`](https://github.com/lawfullyillegal-droid/medium-of-exchange)

#### Legal Definition
> Money is legally defined as a **medium of exchange** - any item or verifiable record that is generally accepted as payment for goods and services and repayment of debts.

#### Statutory References
- **UCC ¬ß 1-201(24)**: "Money" means a medium of exchange currently authorized or adopted by a domestic or foreign government
- **12 USC ¬ß 411**: Federal Reserve notes shall be obligations of the United States
- **31 USC ¬ß 5103**: United States coins and currency are legal tender
- **Article I, Section 8**: Congress has power to coin money and regulate the value thereof
- **Article I, Section 10**: No state shall make anything but gold and silver coin a tender in payment of debts

#### Types of Money
1. **Commodity Money**: Has intrinsic value (gold, silver)
2. **Fiat Money**: Government-issued currency without intrinsic value
3. **Credit Money**: Represents a claim on future money (checks, promissory notes)
4. **Electronic Money**: Digital representation of currency
